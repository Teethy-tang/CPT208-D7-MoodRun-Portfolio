Sketching Insights

  • Users need a very fast entry interaction (under 10 seconds).
  • Emotion adaptation should remain visible but not distracting during runs.
  • Post-run reflection can be playful without adding cognitive burden.

Design Alternatives

Compare 2-3 approaches and justify the selected direction.

Alternative A: Competitive Challenge

  • Strength: Strong motivation for high-performing runners.
  • Weakness: Increases pressure for low-confidence users.
  • Decision: Rejected as the core concept.

Alternative B: Mindfulness-First Running

  • Strength: Supports stress recovery and calm pacing.
  • Weakness: Too passive for users seeking progress.
  • Decision: Partially adopted for tone design.

Selected Direction: MoodRun Hybrid

  • Combines adaptability, gentle encouragement, and progress visibility.
  • Balances wellbeing needs and exercise continuity.
  • Supports both low-energy recovery and high-energy challenge states.
